Do-It-Yourself Bra Sizing


 

Forget the measuring tape. Figure out your bra size yourself by looking for symptoms. How long has it been since you’ve changed your bra size? Many factors in your life can affect a change in your bra size – pregnancy, hormones, weight fluctuations, exercise, etc. So, throughout your life, you can experience 6 or more bra sizes. All things considered, it’s probably worth your while to give some thought to possibly changing the size bra you are wearing.

You have the judgment to figure out your bra size. Going to a store and having a bra fitter determine your size for you can be a mistake – many size incorrectly, don’t put you in a bra size that’s comfortable, or stick you in a bra size that is in stock rather than in your correct size. You, yes YOU are the best person to figure out your bra size. After all, you’re the one who’s going to have to wear this bra size every day. So, simply start with your present bra size, assess the things about your current bra size that you don’t like, and select a new size. HerRoom can help you with this. Here are the 2 simple steps you need to take:

Step 1 – Find your best fitting bra and put it on. If you like the fit, that’s your bra size (as long as that bra’s not old and stretched out), You are done! If you don’t like its fit, go to step 2.

Step 2 – What don’t you like about your best fitting bra? Stand in front of a mirror, and make a list. Now, take this list and compare it to our list of “Solutions To Bra Fit Problems”. Read our recommendations and adjust your bra size to fix what you don’t like. It’s as simple as that!
